Law 1: Never Outshine the Master

To please, do not exaggerate your talents; make the master seem more brilliant.

But if he is weak, discreetly hasten his downfall. However, if he is very weak, let time handle that fall so you don't taint your image.

Law 2: Never Trust Friends Completely—Learn to Use Enemies

People close to you can easily become envious and bring you down because of their access to you. Be careful.

Close enemies, on the other hand, keep you alert and work harder because they need to prove themselves.

Law 3: Conceal Your Intentions

When your plans are hidden, people cannot harm you with preemptive actions and bad intentions.

However, beware of constant smoke screens that get discovered and bring an image of charlatanism.

If necessary, admit the error and show yourself repentant to regain the public's trust.

Law 4: Always Say Less Than Necessary

The more you say, the more common you seem and the more likely you are to say foolish things. Make things imprecise, broad, and enigmatic to seem original and intriguing.

However, being vague is not always wise, especially to avoid fostering hypotheses prejudicial to you or suspicion and insecurity in others.

Be cautious when using this law.

Law 5: So Much Depends on Reputation—Guard It With Your Life

Know what reputation you want to build and act consciously to construct and maintain it according to your desires.

Do not let others build your reputation or you will be at their mercy.

Law 6: Court Attention at All Costs

Stand out and become visible at any price, but be careful not to appear overly eager for attention, as this transmits insecurity, which repels power.

Law 7: Get Others to Do the Work and Take the Credit

Use the wisdom, knowledge, and physical effort of others for your own cause.

However, for this use to be effective, your position must be unshakeable, or you will be accused of fraud.

Know when sharing credit can be useful to you.

Law 8: Make Other People Come to You—Use Bait If Necessary

It is always better to make your adversary come to you, abandoning their own plans in the process.

Use bait of fabulous gains and attract people to the battlefield that you control.

However, if an opportunity arises to attack in a way that quickly defeats your enemies, do it.

Law 9: Win Through Your Actions, Not Through Arguments

Winning through argument brings resentment and ill will from people. It is better to make others agree with you through your actions, without saying a word.

Only resort to persuasive speech when you need to save your skin, and in that case, even knowing you are lying, convey complete conviction, with the right emotion and adequate confidence.

Law 10: Avoid the Unhappy and the Unlucky

Associate with the happy and fortunate, as emotional states are as contagious as diseases.

The unhappy sometimes create their own misfortune; they will create yours too.

Law 11: Learn to Keep People Dependent on You

The more people depend on you, the more freedom you will have.

Make people depend on you for their happiness and prosperity, and you will have nothing to fear.

But remember that all independence has its price; learn to deal with that pressure so you don't succumb.

Law 12: Use Selective Honesty and Generosity to Disarm People

One sincere and honest gesture will cover dozens of dishonest ones. Even the most suspicious people lower their guard in the face of frank and generous actions.

However, when you already have a history of dissimulation, there is no honesty, generosity, or kindness that can deceive people.

Law 13: When Asking for Help, Appeal to Self-Interest, Never to Mercy or Gratitude

If you need to ask for help from an ally, don't bother reminding them of your assistance and good deeds in the past. Instead, reveal something in your request or alliance with them that will benefit them, and exaggerate the emphasis.

However, remember that some people prefer any opportunity to display how good they are. In that case, give them that.

Law 14: Pose as a Friend, Work as a Spy

Use spies to gather precious information that will put you one step ahead and help you better know your rival. Better yet: play the spy yourself.

But remember that just as you spy, others will spy on you too. So if necessary, spread some lies to protect yourself.

Law 15: Crush Your Enemy Totally

The dangerous enemy must be crushed completely, both physically and spiritually.

If even one ember remains, however small, it will eventually turn into a fire.

However, if you have the chance to let him destroy himself or even promote his self-destruction, even better.

Law 16: Use Absence to Increase Respect and Honor

Excessive circulation causes prices to fall: the more you are seen and heard, the more common you will seem.

You must know when to withdraw. Create value through scarcity.

However, this law only applies if you have already reached a certain level of power. Withdraw too early and you will be forgotten.

Law 17: Keep Others in Suspense—Cultivate an Air of Unpredictability

Your predictability gives them a sense of control. Turn the tables: be deliberately unpredictable to leave your enemies disoriented.

But use predictability when you need to make people around you feel comfortable and calm so you can direct them where you want.

Law 18: Don't Build Fortresses to Protect Yourself—Isolation Is Dangerous

Isolation exposes you to more danger than it protects you from. You become isolated from valuable information, turn into an easy and obvious target.

Better to circulate among people, discover allies, and blend in. The crowd serves as a shield against your enemies.

However, isolation may be necessary for more detailed planning without confusing external influences, but use it for a short time to not lose sight of life's reality.

Law 19: Know Who You're Dealing With—Don't Offend the Wrong Person

Deceive or trick certain people and they will spend the rest of their lives seeking revenge on you.

Therefore, be careful in choosing your victims and adversaries and never offend or deceive the wrong person.

Law 20: Don't Commit Yourself to Anyone

Don't commit yourself to parties or causes, only to yourself.

But remember that people will demand a party from you. Know when to choose a side for appearances and gain more allies.

Law 21: Play a Sucker to Catch a Sucker—Seem Foolish to Disarm People

The trick is to make people feel smart, and not just smart, but smarter than you.

Once convinced of this, they will never suspect you might have ulterior motives.

Only pay attention when you are rising in power, a moment when it's worth discretely showing your intelligence and abilities above the competition.

Law 22: Use the Surrender Tactic: Transform Weakness Into Power

If you are the weakest, don't fight just for a matter of honor; it's better to surrender. By surrendering, you have time to recover, time to torment and irritate the conqueror, time to wait for him to lose his power.

Law 23: Concentrate Your Forces

Keep your forces concentrated and unified. Dispersing your energy across multiple fronts weakens you.

An army united under a single command is much more effective than multiple forces spread out.

Law 24: Play the Perfect Courtesan

Show that you dedicate yourself completely to your objectives and that nothing is perfect enough for you.

This sets you apart from others and places you in a position of prominence, as long as you don't become unbearable with this attitude.

Law 25: Reinvent Yourself

You are defined by the actions you take, so choose them carefully to shape your image.

If you don't control your public image, others will, and probably in a way harmful to you.

Law 26: Keep Your Hands Clean

Never dirty yourself with unpleasant or illegal tasks. Use other people for that.

Keep distance from problems so you can never be pointed to as responsible.

Law 27: Play on People's Need to Believe, Create a Cult Following

Use fear strategically to keep people under your control.

Ignorance feeds fear, so keep certain information hidden to create an aura of mystery.

Law 28: Enter Action With Boldness

Hesitation and indecision are signs of weakness. Acting with conviction and boldness demonstrates power.

If you have to choose between doing something risky or doing nothing, it's often better to act.

Law 29: Plan All the Way to the End

Always have a plan until the final conclusion. People who plan to the end can anticipate obstacles.

Improvisation may work in the short term, but true victory comes from meticulous planning.

Law 30: Make Your Accomplishments Seem Effortless

When you make something difficult seem easy, people think you are naturally talented.

The hard work behind an action should remain hidden. However, there are also times when it's worth revealing the effort of your projects.

It all depends on your audience's taste and the era you operate in.

Law 31: Control the Options—Get Others to Play With the Cards You Deal

Make it seem like people have options, so they think they are in control.

Give people options that always generate results favorable to you.

However, there are situations where it's more advantageous to give people more freedom. Seeing them act, you have the opportunity to spy, gather information, and get new ideas.

Law 32: Play to People's Fantasies

In general, truth is avoided because it is ugly and unpleasant.

Life is so hard and anguishing that people capable of creating novels or invoking fantasies are like oases in the middle of the desert: everyone rushes there.

There is enormous power in awakening the fantasy of the masses, but also great risk in awakening this kind of passion. Act with caution.

Law 33: Discover Each Person's Weakness

Everyone has a weak point that is generally an insecurity, an emotion, an uncontrollable need, or sometimes a little secret pleasure.

Whatever it may be, once you find that weak point, that's where you should apply pressure.

However, remember that taking advantage of another's weakness can trigger actions you won't be able to control.

Law 34: Be Royal in Your Own Fashion—Act Like a King to Be Treated Like One

The way you behave generally determines how you are treated. By acting with royalty and confidence in your powers, you show yourself destined to wear a crown.

However, be careful with exaggeration. It's not good to be too far above the crowd to avoid creating repulsion, nor to become an easy target.

Law 35: Master the Art of Timing

Always show yourself patient, as if you knew that everything would eventually come to you.

Sniff out the spirit of the times and the trends that will lead you to power.

Learn to wait when it's not yet time, and attack fiercely when it's opportune.

Law 36: Disdain What You Cannot Have—Ignore It Is the Best Revenge

The more attention you give to something, the stronger you make it. Sometimes it's better to leave things as they are.

If there is something you want but cannot have, show disdain. The less interest you reveal, the more superior you will seem.

However, be careful with disdain. Some situations resolve themselves, but there are those that really do need attention and care.

Law 37: Create Compelling Spectacles

Surprising images and great symbols create an aura of power and everyone reacts to them.

Stage spectacles for those around you, filled with interesting visual elements and radiant symbols that enhance your presence.

Dazzled by appearances, no one will notice what you're really doing.

Law 38: Think as You Like, But Behave Like Others

If you boast that you are contrary to the trends of the era, displaying your unconventional ideas and unorthodox ways, people will think you're just trying to get attention and judge yourself superior.

They will find a way to punish you for making them feel inferior. So it's much safer to join them and develop a common touch.

Share your originality only with tolerant friends, those who will surely appreciate your uniqueness, or when your power reaches a level that allows this kind of behavior without you losing power.

Law 39: Stir Up Waters to Catch Fish

Anger and emotional reactions are counterproductive from a strategic standpoint. You need to stay calm and objective. But if you can irritate the enemy without losing your composure, you gain an undeniable advantage.

Throw the enemy off balance: discover a breach in his vanity to confuse him and you gain control.

However, be careful when playing with people's emotions. Sometimes it's better to leave some fish at the bottom of the lake.

Law 40: Despise the Free Lunch

What is offered for free is dangerous—usually it's a trick or has a hidden obligation.

If something has value, it's worth paying the full price and owing nothing to anyone.

But remember that people are essentially lazy and prefer everything to fall into their lap rather than having to work. If it's you, promise easy gains and they won't see anything else.

Law 41: Avoid Standing in the Shadows of a Great Man

Don't get lost in the shadow of other famous people who came before you, or trapped in a past that wasn't your doing: establish your own name and identity by changing course.

If you see an opportunity, appropriate things from the past that can bring you closer to power, but after that disdain the legacy and establish yourself in power with your own light.

Law 42: Strike the Shepherd and the Sheep Will Scatter

In general, the source of problems can be in a single individual: the agitator, the arrogant subordinate, the poisoner of goodwill.

Don't wait for the problems they cause to multiply and don't try to negotiate with them. Neutralize their influence by isolating or banishing them.

But remember to do this in a cunning way so the agitator doesn't have time to create counter-actions and end up bringing you down.

Law 43: Win Hearts and Minds

Coercion provokes reactions that end up working against you.

You need to attract people so they willingly come to you, because the seduced person becomes a loyal pawn.

Law 44: Disarm and Infuriate With the Mirror Effect

When you mirror your enemies, acting exactly as they act, they don't understand your strategy. The mirror effect ridicules and humiliates them, causing them to react excessively.

However, be careful with the kind of memory the reflection can provoke in people, which can destroy you. Be very careful when using this law.

Law 45: Preach Change, But Don't Change Too Much at Once

Theoretically, everyone knows change is necessary, but in practice people are creatures of habit. Too much innovation is traumatic and leads to rebellion.

If you are new in a position of power, or an outsider trying to build your power base, explicitly show that you respect the old way of doing things.

If change is necessary, make it seem like a smooth improvement of the past. Just be careful not to associate yourself with a disastrous past whose end is already expected by people.

Law 46: Never Appear Too Perfect

Appearing better than others is always dangerous, but what is extremely dangerous is appearing to have no flaws or weaknesses.

Occasionally display some harmless defects and vices to divert attention and seem more human and accessible.

Only be perfect before the envious ones trying to destroy you; against them your perfection can be fired with precision and cunning.

Law 47: Do Not Go Past the Mark You Have Set—Know When to Stop

The moment of victory is almost always the most dangerous.

In the heat of victory, arrogance and overconfidence can make you advance beyond your goal and, by going too far, you gain more enemies than you defeat.

Be relentless with your enemy, but don't create others by advancing beyond what's necessary. Set the goal and, once reached, stop.

Law 48: Assume Formlessness

Rather than assume a form your enemy can grab onto, keep yourself malleable and in motion.

The best way to protect yourself is to be as fluid and amorphous as water; don't bet on stability or permanent order.

Because everything changes.
